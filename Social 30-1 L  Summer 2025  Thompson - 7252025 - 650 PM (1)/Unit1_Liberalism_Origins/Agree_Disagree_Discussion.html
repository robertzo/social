Discussion Post - Who would agree and disagree?

Introduction

Discussion posts will be used in this course to allow you to state your ideas and also to see the ideas of your classmates. By making your learning visible, your responses will get better as you consider different perspectives that you may not have thought about. Once you have posted a discussion response, you may go back and add to it or post again after looking at the responses of others.

Instructions

  1. Open a Google or Word doc and answer the question below in reference to each source (scroll down to view sources).

    Question

    Who would agree and who would disagree with the following sources? Consider Philosophers, Governments of the Nation States, Collectives/Groups, Political Parties, and your knowledge of Social Studies.

  2. Your answer should be in paragraph form. and no more than 600 words.
  3. Use proper spelling and capitalization.
  4. Once you have completed the submission steps below, you may go back and add to your response or post again after looking at the responses of others.

Submission

For this task, you will be posting it in two locations: Assignments and Discussions.

  1. Go to Assignments and submit your response to the folder "Assignment - Unit 1: Who would agree and disagree"
  2. Go to "Discussions" on the navbar
  3. Go to the forum "Class Discussions"
  4. Click the Discussion Topic called "Unit 1: Who would agree and disagree?"
  5. Click the button "Start a New Thread"
  6. Copy and paste the text from your Word doc or Google doc and then click "Post"

Academic Honesty Rules

  • It is required for you to complete this assignment independently.
  • This is an open-book assignment.
  • Use citations if you will use or present the work of others.
  • All work should be original (cannot be something previously submitted). If your teacher has questions about the originality of your submission they will contact you for further clarification of learning.
  • You may not share your assignment with any person or website.
